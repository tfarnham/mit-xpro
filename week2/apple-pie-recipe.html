French Apple Pie Recipe

This apple pie has been a staple of Thanksgiving for as long as I can remember. I think the original recipe originated from a Betty Crocker cookbook, but has developed over the years

With a flakey crust and crumble top, the apple pie makes for a delicious rustic dessert

Build the crust

  • 10 oz. flour
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 6 oz. shortening
  • 1 3/4 - 2 oz. ice cold water

Make the crust

  1. Add the flour, shortening, and salt to a large bowl
  2. Chop the shortening into small pieces
  3. With your hands, "sheet" the shortening between your hands to form flakes
  4. Add the ice water and mix until just combined
  5. Cut and fold the dough until it comes together and can be rolled to form the crust

Build the filling

  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/2 tsp. nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1 dash salt
  • 6 cups Granny Smith apples
  • a few drops of lemon juice

Make the filling

  1. Peel and core the Granny Smith apples
  2. Thinly slice the apples and toss into cold water with the lemon juice
  3. Mix all the dry ingredients
  4. Drain the apples and mix the dry ingredients with the apples

Build the topping and bake

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup firm butter
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed

Finish the pie!

  1. Mix the topping with two forks until crumbly
  2. Add the apple mixture to the crust
  3. Sprinkle the topping over the apples
  4. Bake at 425ÂºF for 50 minutes, or until golden brown
